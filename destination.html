Best Time to Visit

Timing your safari is crucial for the best wildlife viewing experience. The Indian jungle changes dramatically with the seasons.

  • October to February: Pleasant weather, lush green forests, great for bird watching and general safaris.
  • March to June: Hot and dry, vegetation is sparse making this the absolute best time for tiger and big cat sightings around waterholes.
  • July to September: Most parks are closed due to the monsoon season.

Destination FAQs

Common questions about visiting the national parks.

Are tiger sightings guaranteed?

No, safaris are unpredictable and wildlife is wild. However, our expert guides track alarm calls and pugmarks to give you the highest possible chance of a sighting.

Can children go on safaris?

Yes, children above 5 years are welcome on most jeep safaris. We recommend consulting with us so we can tailor the duration to suit younger travelers.

What should I wear?

Wear muted, earthy tones like khaki, olive, and brown. Avoid bright colors and strong perfumes. Dress in layers as early mornings can be quite cold.

Safari Travel Tips

Photography Gear

Bring a good zoom lens (at least 300mm) and extra memory cards. Bean bags are better than tripods in a jeep.

Silence is Golden

Keep your voice down during the safari. Animals are sensitive to noise and sudden movements.

Carry ID Proof

Original ID cards (Passport/Aadhar) used during booking must be carried for entry at the park gates.
